Lineage Core

Simulation Systems Reference

This document describes the active simulation mechanics in the C# core: the order systems run, the important calculations they perform, and practical examples for reading or tuning a run.

The simulation is a continuous 2D world advanced by fixed ticks. Godot, the CLI, and the launcher configure or visualize the world, but the rules below live in src/Lineage.Core.

Default dt: 1/30 sec Brain inputs: 235 Brain outputs: 10 Canonical biomes: 8

Tick Pipeline

Each call to Simulation.Step() runs the configured systems in order, then advances the clock by dt. The default fixed step is 1/30 second, so 30 ticks equal one simulated second.

Neural Pipeline

1

2
MetabolismSystem: age, cooldown, basal and trait upkeep.
3
SpatialIndexRebuildSystem: resource, egg, and creature query grid.
4
CreatureSensingSystem: internal, contact, vision, scent, terrain, sound.
5
NeuralControllerSystem: evaluate brain or reuse cached action.
6
CreatureGrabSystem: apply creature holds and movement pressure.
7
MovementSystem: move, collide with obstacles, charge movement energy.
8
CreatureSpatialIndexRebuildSystem: refresh moved creature positions.
9

10

11

12

13

14

16

20

Simple Pipeline

The simple pipeline is still available for controlled ecology tests. It replaces sensing plus neural control plus grab/attack with SimpleForagingSystem.

target score = proximity * digestionEfficiency
proximity = 1 - clamp(edgeDistance / senseRadius, 0, 1)

If food is visible in the cone, the creature turns directly toward the best target and moves at effective max speed. Otherwise it wanders at 0.25x speed with deterministic heading jitter.

Why the order matters: eating happens after movement, digestion happens after eating, reproduction happens after digestion, and fat storage plus healing happen before death. In the full neural pipeline, a creature can eat, digest, lay an egg, attack, release fat, heal, and die in the same simulated tick if the conditions line up.

World, Maps, Biomes

Worlds are rectangular continuous spaces with hard bounds. Positions are floating point, while biome and obstacle maps are coarse grids layered over the world.

Spatial Index

Local queries use a uniform grid. The default spatial cell size is 64. Resources and eggs are only rebuilt when their versions change; creature positions are rebuilt before sensing and again after movement.

cellX = floor(position.X / spatialCellSize)
cellY = floor(position.Y / spatialCellSize)
query scans cells intersecting the requested radius plus target radius

Canonical Biomes

The current canonical biome list is Desert, Scrubland, Grassland, Fertile, Forest, Wetland, Tundra, and Highland. Legacy names still load: Barren means Desert, Sparse means Scrubland, and Rich means Fertile.

Biome Pressure Profiles

Scenarios provide per-biome multipliers for movement energy cost, basal cost, speed, vision range, and seasonal amplitude. Any biome pressure profile is a simple lookup:

effectivePressure = profile.For(currentBiome)

For example, if Wetland speed is 0.68, a creature asking for 20 px/s moves at 13.6 px/s before obstacle resolution.

World Generation

Biome maps can be uniform, generated noise, natural climate, several band/corridor layouts, or manual/reusable map artifacts. Natural climate generation computes deterministic elevation, moisture, temperature, forest fitness, and fertility fields from seeded fractal noise, then ranks cells into ecological categories and smooths regions.

temperature = clamp01(latitudeTemperature * 0.76 + temperatureNoise * 0.25 - elevation * 0.12 + 0.04)
moisture = clamp01(moistureNoise * 0.78 + detail * 0.14 + (1 - elevation) * 0.14 - rainShadow)
fertility = clamp01(moisture * 0.42 + temperateFitness * 0.28 + (1 - elevation) * 0.18 + detail * 0.08 - wetnessPenalty)

Resources And Seasons

Resources are either plants or meat. Plants are long-lived patches that regrow or enter dormancy after depletion. Meat is created by creature deaths and decays over time.

Plant Regrowth

fertilityMultiplier = seasonalMultiplier * localFertilityMultiplier
calories = min(maxCalories, calories + regrowthCaloriesPerSecond * fertilityMultiplier * dt)

When plants are depleted and respawn delay is enabled, they enter dormancy. Dormancy countdown is also scaled by fertility, so a plant in a fertile season returns faster than a plant in a poor season.

Local Fertility

Local fertility is short-lived depletion pressure, separate from biome identity. A depleted plant reduces its cell by depletionPerPlant, adjacent cardinal cells by neighborDepletionShare, and diagonal cells by half that neighbor share. Recovery adds recoveryPerSecond * dt until the cell reaches 1.0.

centerCell = max(minimumMultiplier, centerCell - depletionPerPlant)
neighborCell = max(minimumMultiplier, neighborCell - depletionPerPlant * neighborShare)
cell = min(1, cell + recoveryPerSecond * dt)

Seasonal Fertility

phase = ((elapsedSeconds + phaseOffset + spatialOffset) mod seasonLength) / seasonLength
seasonalMultiplier = max(0, 1 + amplitude * sin(phase * tau))
effectiveAmplitude = clamp(baseAmplitude * biomeAmplitudeMultiplier, 0, 0.95)

Horizontal or vertical opposed season modes offset one half of the world by half a season, creating simultaneous good and bad regions.

Meat Decay

meatAge += dt
freshKillSecondsRemaining = max(0, freshKillSecondsRemaining - dt)
calories -= decayCaloriesPerSecond * dt

Meat is removed when calories reach zero. Fresh-kill attribution expires when the fresh-kill timer reaches zero.

Growth

maturityProgress = maturityAge <= 0 ? 1 : clamp(age / maturityAge, 0, 1)
smooth = progress * progress * (3 - 2 * progress)
newbornScale = clamp(0.35 * juvenileGrowthScale(birthInvestmentRatio), 0.18, 0.7)
growthFactor = newbornScale + (1 - newbornScale) * smooth

Body, eating, gut, fat capacity, digestion, bite, and resistance scale directly by growthFactor. Speed, turning, and sense radius scale by sqrt(growthFactor); speed also receives the current fat burden multiplier. Vision angle is not scaled by growth.

Example: a baseline-investment juvenile halfway to maturity has progress = 0.5, smooth = 0.5, newbornScale = 0.35, and growthFactor = 0.675. Its body radius is 3 * 0.675 = 2.025, while max speed is 24 * sqrt(0.675) = 19.7 px/s.

Investment Scaling

investmentRatio = clamp(investedEnergy / baselineOffspringInvestment, 0.25, 4)
eggMaxHealth = clamp(sqrt(investmentRatio), 0.5, 2)
hatchlingHealth = clamp(sqrt(investmentRatio) * eggHealthRatio, 0.1, 2)
juvenileGrowthScale = clamp(sqrt(investmentRatio), 0.5, 2)

Metabolism

Metabolism updates age, meal timers, reproduction cooldown, and passive energy loss.

traitUpkeep = sum(effectiveTrait * matchingTraitCost)
traitUpkeep += plantSpecializationFactor * plantSpecializationCost
traitUpkeep += clamp(memoryVector.length, 0, 1) * memoryCost
traitUpkeep += rtNeatHiddenNodes * 0.001 + rtNeatEnabledConnections * 0.00025
energy -= (basalEnergyPerSecond * biomeBasalMultiplier + traitUpkeep) * dt

Plant specialization upkeep uses squared typed plant adaptations:

plantSpecializationFactor = tenderAdaptation^2 + richAdaptation^2 + toughAdaptation^2
Example: if basal is 0.25/s, current biome basal multiplier is 1.2, trait upkeep is 0.05/s, and dt = 1/30, the tick drains (0.25 * 1.2 + 0.05) / 30 = 0.0117 energy.

rtNEAT graph brains add topology upkeep by default: 0.001/s for each hidden node and 0.00025/s for each enabled connection. Dense Hybrid and HiddenLayer brains do not use that topology-cost path yet.

Fat Storage

Fat storage is an automatic late-pipeline reserve system. It stores surplus working energy as fat when a creature is well above its reproduction threshold, and releases stored fat when working energy falls into starvation pressure.

Reserve Capacity And Burden

effectiveFatCapacity = genome.FatStorageCapacityCalories * growthFactor
fatStorageRatio = capacity > 0 ? clamp(fatCalories / capacity, 0, 1) : 0
fatMassBurdenRatio = clamp(fatCalories / max(1, reproductionEnergyThreshold), 0, 1)
fatSpeedMultiplier = clamp(1 - fatMassBurdenRatio * 0.12, 0.78, 1)
fatMovementCostMultiplier = 1 + fatMassBurdenRatio * 0.25

Effective max speed includes fatSpeedMultiplier, and movement energy cost includes fatMovementCostMultiplier.

Deposit And Release

Defaults are deposit ratio 1.25, withdraw ratio 0.25, and transfer capacity share 0.45/s.

transferLimit = effectiveFatCapacity * transferCapacitySharePerSecond * dt
withdrawTarget = reproductionEnergyThreshold * withdrawEnergyRatio
if energy < withdrawTarget:
  fatToBurn = min(fatCalories, (withdrawTarget - energy) / efficiency, transferLimit)
  energy += fatToBurn * efficiency
  fatCalories -= fatToBurn

depositTarget = reproductionEnergyThreshold * depositEnergyRatio
if energy > depositTarget:
  energyToStore = min(energy - depositTarget, (capacity - fatCalories) / efficiency, transferLimit)
  energy -= energyToStore
  fatCalories += energyToStore * efficiency
Example: with threshold 84, withdraw target is 21 energy and deposit target is 105. A creature at 15 energy with 20 fat and 0.85 efficiency needs 6 usable energy, so it burns about 7.06 fat if the transfer limit allows it.

Healing

Healing is an automatic late-pipeline recovery mechanic. It starts only after a creature has avoided attack and rotten-meat damage for a configurable cooldown, then restores health slowly while spending energy.

Defaults And Formula

Defaults are delay 25s, healing rate 0.006 of max health per second, energy cost 3 per health restored, and minimum energy floor 30.

if attackDamageTaken > 0 or rottenMeatDamage > 0: secondsSinceDamage = 0
secondsSinceDamage += dt
if secondsSinceDamage >= healingDelay and energy > healingMinimumEnergy:
  healByRate = healingHealthFractionPerSecond * maxHealth * dt
  healByEnergy = (energy - healingMinimumEnergy) / healingEnergyCostPerHealth
  healing = min(missingHealth, healByRate, healByEnergy)
  health += healing
  energy -= healing * healingEnergyCostPerHealth
Example: an adult with max health 1 heals at 0.006/s after the cooldown. Restoring 0.1 health takes about 16.7s of uninterrupted healing and costs 0.3 energy.

Sensing

Sensing converts world state plus internal creature state into compact neural inputs. The expensive world portion can be skipped for several ticks and reused unless a close contact or other cue forces refresh.

World Sense Refresh

forced if worldSenseIntervalTicks <= 1, tick == 0, or no prior sense tick
close refresh if touching food, touching creature, movement blocked, or high proximity
scheduled refresh if (tick + creatureId) mod worldSenseIntervalTicks == 0
otherwise reuse previous world senses and age them

The default world sense interval is 10 ticks. Close refresh uses a default proximity threshold of 0.85 and a minimum age of 1 tick.

Internal Senses

energyRatio = clamp(energy / reproductionEnergyThreshold, 0, 1)
healthRatio = clamp(health / maxJuvenileHealth, 0, 1)
hunger = 1 - energyRatio
eggReserveRatio = clamp(reproductiveEnergy / offspringInvestment, 0, 1)
energySurplusRatio = clamp((energy - reproductionThreshold) / max(1, offspringInvestment), 0, 1)

Recent food, digestion, and typed plant payoff traces provide short-term feedback. Plant payoff traces decay with a half-life of 45 seconds by default:

decay = 0.5 ^ (dt / halfLifeSeconds)
newTrace = clamp(max(oldTrace * decay, immediateYield), 0, 1)

Vision

A target is visible when it is within edge range and inside the vision cone:

insideCone = visionAngle >= tau || dot(directionToTarget, forward) >= cos(visionAngle / 2)
proximity = 1 - clamp(edgeDistance / effectiveVisionRadius, 0, 1)
visibleDensity = clamp(visibleCount / 8, 0, 1)

Optional sector vision samples 9 ordered sectors from left to right across the vision arc. Each sector has 18 channels: plant, meat, egg, creature, smaller/similar/larger creature density and proximity, plant quality/ease, creature approach, and facing alignment.

Scent And Sound

Meat scent uses the effective sense radius times a scent multiplier, default 2x. Sound uses a default 2.5x range multiplier.

distanceFactor = 1 - clamp(edgeDistance / scentRadius, 0, 1)
meatScentStrength = clamp(calories / 60, 0, 1) * distanceFactor^2
density = clamp(totalStrength / densitySaturation, 0, 1)
directionalConfidence = clamp(vectorLength / totalStrength, 0, 1) * density

Rotten meat scent is meat scent multiplied by staleness. Creature similarity scent is weighted by genetic similarity and has a 1.5x sense-radius range. Sound strength is emitted sound amplitude times squared distance falloff; tone is the weighted average of heard tones, and tone clarity falls as nearby tones conflict.

Brains And Actions

The current neural adapter exposes 235 inputs and 10 outputs. Dense brains store direct input-to-output weights plus optional hidden nodes. Every evaluated dense output is passed through tanh.

Evaluation

directOutput[o] = sum(input[i] * directWeight[o, i])
hidden[h] = tanh(sum(input[i] * hiddenInputWeight[h, i]))
output[o] = tanh(directOutput[o] + sum(hidden[h] * hiddenOutputWeight[o, h]))

Hybrid neural brains support direct weights plus hidden nodes. Hidden-layer neural brains keep the same payload layout for compatibility but force direct weights to zero so all behavior routes through hidden nodes. The visible starter catalog uses Hybrid 4 and Hidden 16 dense profiles for the main roles.

With 235 inputs and 10 outputs, direct weights alone use 2350 floats. A 4-hidden-node hybrid starter stores 2350 + 4*235 + 4*10 = 3330 weights. A generic 10-hidden-node hidden-layer brain stores 2350 + 10*235 + 10*10 = 4700 weights, though the direct block is inactive for hidden-layer behavior. A Hidden 16 catalog starter stores 6270 weights, with 3920 active hidden-path weights.

rtNEAT Graphs

RtNeatGraph brains use the same semantic inputs and outputs as dense brains, but store explicit input, hidden, and output nodes plus enabled/disabled weighted connections. Mutations can perturb weights and hidden-node biases, add or remove valid connections, insert hidden nodes by splitting connections, mutate hidden activation/bias parameters, and prune inactive hidden nodes.

topologyUpkeepPerSecond = hiddenNodeCount * 0.001 + enabledConnectionCount * 0.00025

Reports and stats include rtNEAT topology telemetry, and the HTML report can show representative living graph brains. Current rtNEAT is a one-parent sparse graph mutation architecture; it is not full NEAT crossover with innovation-number speciation.

Action Reuse And Memory

Optional neural action reuse skips brain evaluation when world senses are stale. It still updates movement and memory from cached actions. Reuse is broken by fresh world sense, first decision, contact, blocked movement, attack damage, grab state, rotten meat damage, internal energy/hunger/reproduction deltas of 0.2, health drop of 0.05, or max reuse age.

memory *= exp(-memoryDecayPerSecond * dt)
writeVector = forward * memoryForward + right * memoryRight
blend = 1 - exp(-memoryWriteRatePerSecond * dt)
memory = clampLength(memory * (1 - blend) + desiredMemory * blend, 1)

Movement

The brain writes desired velocity indirectly through move and turn outputs. Movement then applies grab pressure, biome speed, bounds, obstacles, and movement energy cost.

desiredVelocity = clampLength(creature.DesiredVelocity, effectiveMaxSpeed)
desiredVelocity *= grabMovementMultiplier
terrainAdjustedVelocity = desiredVelocity * biomeSpeedMultiplier
intendedPosition = boundsClamp(previousPosition + terrainAdjustedVelocity * dt)

Obstacle resolution first tries the intended position, then x-only movement, then y-only movement, then stays at the previous position.

speedCostMultiplier = speed <= 0 ? 0 : (speed / baselineMaxSpeed) ^ movementSpeedCostExponent
energy -= movementEnergyPerSecond * biomeMovementCostMultiplier * growthFactor * fatMovementCostMultiplier * speedCostMultiplier * dt
Example: an adult baseline creature moving at 12 px/s with exponent 1 has speed multiplier 12 / 24 = 0.5. With movement energy 0.35/s, neutral biome, and dt=1/30, movement costs 0.35 * 0.5 / 30 = 0.0058 energy that tick.

Eating And Digestion

Eating transfers raw calories into gut stores. Digestion later turns those raw calories into energy according to diet, plant type, meat freshness, and carrion adaptation.

Eating Contact And Target Choice

contactRadius = effectiveBodyRadius + reachPadding
availableGutCapacity = max(0, effectiveGutCapacity - gutPlantCalories - gutMeatCalories)

The best touching food is the highest digestion-efficiency candidate, with ties broken by smaller edge distance and then smaller center distance. Eggs count as meat-like food.

plantAmount = min(resourceCalories, effectiveEatRate * plantEatRateMultiplier * dt, availableGutCapacity)
meatAmount = min(resourceCalories, effectiveEatRate * dt, availableGutCapacity)
eggAmount = min(eggEnergy, effectiveEatRate * dt, availableGutCapacity)

Diet Efficiency

plantEfficiency = 0.2 + 0.8 * clamp(1 - dietaryAdaptation, 0, 1)
meatEfficiency = 0.2 + 0.8 * clamp(dietaryAdaptation, 0, 1)
plantTypeEnergy = plantEfficiency * plantDigestionMultiplier * plantTypeAdaptationMultiplier
meatEnergy = meatEfficiency * meatFreshnessEfficiency

Dietary adaptation near 0 favors plants; near 1 favors meat. Typed plant adaptations can improve matching plant types and penalize cross-specialization.

Meat Freshness And Carrion

freshness = 0.35 + 0.65 * (1 - clamp(meatAgeSeconds / 300, 0, 1))
staleFactor = (1 - freshness) / (1 - 0.35)
rottenDamage = meatDigested * rottenDamagePerRawKcal * rottenMeatDamageMultiplier

Carrion adaptation trades fresh-meat efficiency for better stale-meat recovery and rotten-meat protection.

Digestion Budget

digestionBudget = effectiveDigestionCaloriesPerSecond * dt
rawDigested = min(totalGutCalories, digestionBudget)
plantDigested = min(gutPlantCalories, rawDigested * gutPlantCalories / totalGutCalories)
meatDigested = min(gutMeatCalories, rawDigested - plantDigested)
releasedEnergy = plantReleasedEnergy + meatReleasedEnergy
Example: a plant specialist with dietaryAdaptation = 0.1 has plant efficiency 0.2 + 0.8 * 0.9 = 0.92 and meat efficiency 0.2 + 0.8 * 0.1 = 0.28. Ten generic plant raw calories release about 9.2 energy; ten fresh meat calories release about 2.8 energy.

Reproduction

Creatures reproduce by building an egg reserve from surplus energy, then laying an egg when reserve is full and reproduction intent is satisfied.

Reserve Transfer

availableEnergy = max(0, energy - reproductionEnergyThreshold)
remainingEggEnergy = offspringInvestment - reproductiveEnergy
eggProductionRate = eggProductionEnergyPerSecond * fertilityMultiplier
transfer = min(eggProductionRate * dt, availableEnergy, remainingEggEnergy)

Fertility

ageFertility = 0 before maturity
ageFertility = 1 from maturity through prime age
ageFertility = lerp(1, senescentFertilityMultiplier, smoothstep(progress)) after prime age
crowdingFertility = 1 - crowdingPenalty * smoothstep(clamp(visibleCreatureDensity, 0, 1))
fertilityMultiplier = clamp(ageFertility * crowdingFertility, 0, 1)

Default reproductive prime age is 240s, senescence age is 900s, senescent fertility multiplier is 0.18, and crowding penalty is 0.65.

Mutation

Effective mutation pressure is world/scenario-bound at reproduction time. Legacy mutation fields still exist on genomes and species profiles for compatibility, but reproduction uses WorldMutationPolicy.

trait mutates if random() < traitMutationRate
if no trait selected and strength > 0, one random trait is selected
numericTrait = clamp(value * (1 + random(-1, 1) * mutationStrength), min, max)
unitTrait = clamp(value + random(-strength, strength), 0, 1)
brainWeight = clamp(weight + random(-strength, strength), -8, 8)

The current world mutation default is strength 0.06, trait mutation rate 0.20, and brain mutation rate 0.08.

Eggs

Eggs are first-class entities. They store parent, generation, genome, brain, energy, health, max health, investment ratio, incubation time, and birth mutation metadata.

Grab And Combat

Creature interaction currently has two physical mechanisms: grabbing and attacking. Both require brain intent in the neural pipeline.

Grab

A creature can grab a contacted creature, or continue holding a held target within body radii plus padding. Grab strength depends on output and relative body size.

grabPressure = clamp(grabOutput * sqrt(grabberRadius / targetRadius), 0, 1)
targetEffort = clamp(target.MoveForward, 0, 1) + abs(target.Turn) * 0.35
breaks if targetEffort * sqrt(targetRadius / grabberRadius) > grabPressure + 0.65
movementMultiplier = clamp(1 - 0.85 * clamp(grabPressure, 0, 1), 0, 1)
Example: a same-sized grabber with grabOutput = 0.7 applies 0.7 pressure. The grabbed creature's movement multiplier is 1 - 0.85 * 0.7 = 0.405, so a requested 20 px/s movement becomes about 8.1 px/s before biome speed.

Attack

Attacks require a living contact target, attack intent, enough energy to pay bite cost, and target position in the attacker's forward half-arc.

biteCost = baseBiteCostPerSecond * max(0.05, effectiveBiteStrength) * dt
rawDamagePerSecond = baseBiteDamagePerSecond * max(0.05, effectiveBiteStrength) * sqrt(max(0.1, effectiveBodyRadius / baselineBodyRadius))
finalDamage = rawDamagePerSecond * dt / sqrt(max(0.1, effectiveTargetDamageResistance))
Current default combat is intentionally first-pass and can be slow to kill. With base bite damage 0.18/s, adult baseline bite 0.55, body radius 3, and target resistance 1, damage is 0.099/s, or 0.0033 per 30 Hz tick. A full-health target takes about 10.1s of sustained biting to die.

Death And Meat

Death is resolved near the end of the tick. Creatures die from starvation, injury, or rotten meat damage, then become meat resources.

dies if energy <= 0 or health <= 0
meatCalories = effectiveBodyRadius * meatCaloriesPerBodyRadius + (max(0, energy) + max(0, fatCalories)) * meatEnergyFraction
meatRadius = clamp(effectiveBodyRadius * 0.85, 1.5, 12)

Injury deaths can carry attacker attribution. Fresh-kill credit lasts 20s, allowing reports and stats to distinguish live-prey calories from ordinary carcass scavenging.

Stats And History

The run stores event counters continuously and sampled snapshots at statsSnapshotIntervalTicks. The default interval is 300 ticks, which is 10 simulated seconds at 30 Hz. The Long Run Performance recipe uses 54000 ticks, which is 1800 simulated seconds, or 30 minutes.

What Snapshot Interval Changes

DataEffect Of Larger Interval
Time-series snapshotsFewer rows for population, resources, energy, traits, diet, combat, memory, region, and biome graphs.
Per-snapshot ratesRecorded less often. Many rates describe the sampled tick's recent per-creature counters, not an average over all skipped ticks.
Creature exposure heatmapSampled less often. Each sample adds sampleIntervalTicks * dt seconds at the creature's current position and biome.
Event countersBirths, deaths, eggs, plant dormancy, and death causes still accumulate when events happen.
Event heatmapsBirth, death, food eaten, egg eaten, and attack damage heatmaps are event-driven and are not lost just because snapshots are sparse.
Lineage recordsBirth/death records are separate from snapshot interval.

Snapshot Categories

Snapshots include clock/season, population, eggs, plant and meat resources, local fertility, brain counts, hidden-node magnitudes, rtNEAT topology metrics, generation, energy, biome occupancy and deaths, eastward progress, sensing densities, food intake, digestion, gut composition, attack/grab/sound, healing, travel efficiency, reproduction, diet traits, rotten meat, lifespan, memory, and left/middle/right region summaries.

Spatial Heatmaps

Heatmaps use a maximum axis of 48 cells while preserving aspect ratio.

if width >= height: cellCountX = 48, cellCountY = round(48 * height / width)
else: cellCountY = 48, cellCountX = round(48 * width / height)
cellIndex = floor(x / worldWidth * cellCountX) + floor(y / worldHeight * cellCountY) * cellCountX

Stored heatmap layers include births, deaths by cause, plant/meat/egg calories eaten, attack damage, spatial creature exposure seconds, and aggregate biome exposure seconds.

Extinct Payload Pruning

Optional extinct payload pruning removes genome and brain payloads no longer referenced by living creatures, eggs, or survivor ancestry. Lineage records remain, with retained payload IDs remapped to compact arrays.

Species And Catalogs

Species systems are report and catalog layers; they do not change simulation behavior. They help compare evolved populations, export representatives, and seed later runs.

Species Profiles

A species profile is a portable representative creature: name, notes, source metadata, genome, brain architecture, hidden node count, and neural weights. Profiles can also point to a default brain profile path so body and controller can be swapped later.

profile = representative genome + embedded fallback brain + source metadata + optional defaultBrainPath

Cluster Analysis

Current species clusters group living creatures by genome features and brain features. Defaults are genome distance threshold 0.16, brain distance threshold 0.028, combined threshold 0.16, genome weight 0.55, and brain weight 0.45.

combinedDistance = genomeDistance * 0.55 + brainDistance * 0.45
same cluster when genome, brain, and combined distances are all within thresholds

Cluster summaries include living share, founders, representative creature, generation range, average traits, diet label, tactic label, region label, behavior fingerprints, and brain input diagnostics.

Lineage

Every creature receives a lineage record at birth: entity ID, parent ID, birth tick/time/position, generation, genome ID, brain ID, birth energy, effective birth mutation values, and max eastward progress. Death fills in death tick/time/position, death reason, and attacker if applicable.

Worked Examples

One Baseline Tick

Assume a baseline adult in neutral biome at 30 Hz, moving at half speed and not eating this tick.

dt = 1 / 30 = 0.0333s
basal loss = 0.25 * 0.0333 = 0.0083
movement speed = 12 px/s
movement speed multiplier = 12 / 24 = 0.5
movement loss = 0.35 * 0.5 * 0.0333 = 0.0058
total tick loss before trait upkeep = 0.0141 energy

Plant Bite To Energy

A baseline adult touches a tender plant with empty gut for one tick.

effectiveEatRate = 18 kcal/s
tender eat multiplier = 1.5
raw eaten = 18 * 1.5 / 30 = 0.9 kcal

baseline dietaryAdaptation = 0.1
plant efficiency = 0.2 + 0.8 * 0.9 = 0.92
tender digestion multiplier = 0.85
tender plant energy efficiency = 0.92 * 0.85 = 0.782 before typed adaptation

if digestion budget is available, 0.9 raw tender kcal releases about 0.704 energy

Snapshot Storage Choice

At 30 Hz, a 24-hour simulated run is 2,592,000 ticks.

default interval 300 ticks:
2,592,000 / 300 = 8,640 snapshots

long-run interval 54,000 ticks:
2,592,000 / 54,000 = 48 snapshots

The larger interval keeps event counters and event heatmaps, but the population and trait time series become coarse. It is a good long-run starting point when you care about final/tail ecology and do not need minute-by-minute curves.
